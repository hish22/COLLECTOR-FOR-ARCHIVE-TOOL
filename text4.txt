The lighthouse keeper had spent forty years tending to the same rotating beam, watching the rhythmic pulse of light cut through the ink-black ocean night after night. He knew every jagged rock and hidden current along the coastline, yet he had never once set foot on a ship to see what lay beyond the horizon. One morning, a heavy fog rolled in, thick and silent, swallowing the world until the ocean and the sky became indistinguishable. As he stepped out onto the gallery to clear the glass, he found a small, storm-battered bird resting on the railing, its wings shivering. He shielded the creature with his coat, realizing that while he had spent his life keeping others safe from the shore, he had found his own purpose in the quiet, fragile moment of offering sanctuary to a traveler who had finally made it home.
