A weary traveler reached the summit of the mountain just as the sun began to dip below the horizon, painting the sky in shades of bruised purple and burning gold. He had spent months climbing, driven by the rumor that at the very top, one could see the map of their own life laid out in the valleys below. When he finally looked down, however, there were no clear paths or charted destinations—only an endless, shifting sea of clouds that masked the ground completely. He realized then that the journey was never about finding a map to follow, but about proving to himself that he had the strength to climb through the mist, regardless of what lay hidden beneath it.
